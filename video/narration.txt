Gather close, and I'll tell you of Cipherfell.

A quiet little village, where folk kept their secrets on a handshake. But a greedy baron coveted it. And by night, his agents slipped through the gate, wearing borrowed names and forged seals.

So the Duke named a young Warden, and gave but one rule. Trust nothing you cannot prove.

To unmask the stranger at the gate, the Warden learned that a face and a scar cannot be borrowed... though a password can be overheard.

To keep the Duke's letters safe, that the secret lives in the key, never in the message.

To catch a thief in a locked granary, that the fewer keys you hand out, the fewer hands can betray you.

And to stop a baron who knew too much, that a hundred harmless whispers, gathered together, become a secret.

No swords. No magic. Only a careful eye, and the quiet craft of keeping what matters safe.

They called it cybersecurity. The Warden simply called it... paying attention.
